- Final status: resolved

### HR-B05-001 — effect_mapping_boundary

- Question: How should counter placement/removal participate in calculated damage and maximum-threat fields?
- Evidence: Alakazam card:743:attack:0 places 2 damage counters per card in the player's hand.; Munkidori card:112:ability:0 moves up to 3 damage counters and must preserve the same removed/placed amount.; Froslass card:104:ability:0 places one counter on every matching Pokemon during Pokemon Checkup.; Damage counters cause HP loss but are not attack damage and do not use Weakness, Resistance, or ordinary damage modifiers/prevention.
- Recommendation: Store exact counters canonically and derive 10-HP-per-counter values for KO/threat calculations, but keep damage_kind=damage_counters so Weakness, Resistance, and damage-only effects are never applied.
- Human decision: Approved: store exact counter counts canonically and derive 10 HP per counter for KO/threat calculations, while retaining damage_kind=damage_counters so ordinary damage rules never apply.
- Final status: resolved

### HR-B05-002 — stochastic_maximum_policy

- Question: How should credible maximum be represented for attacks with an unbounded but rapidly decaying random tail?
- Evidence: Mega Kangaskhan ex card:756:attack:0 starts at 200 and adds 50 per heads while flipping until the first tails.; The exact expected damage is 250 and the legal theoretical maximum is unbounded.; Damage quantiles are deterministic: p50=200, p90=350, p95=400, p99=500.; The current encoder's reviewed maximum is 200 and loses all heads bonuses.
- Recommendation: Store the exact distribution plus named quantiles and leave credible maximum as that structured profile rather than an arbitrary scalar. A later encoder can select its risk tolerance without losing information.
- Human decision: Approved a conservative practical approximation: enumerate outcomes for 0, 1, 2, and at most 3 consecutive heads; do not calculate outcomes at 4 or more heads for top-meta features. For Mega Kangaskhan ex this yields practical outcomes 200, 250, 300, and optionally 350, with practical ceiling 350. Record the omitted tail explicitly. This is an approved exception to lossless coverage; any other approximation requires separate human review.
- Final status: resolved

### HR-B05-003 — natural_text_engine_order_discrepancy

- Question: Which ordering should the canonical Cursed Blast program preserve when the natural-language presentation and exact competition-engine execution order differ?
- Evidence: The English Cursed Blast text describes placing 5 or 13 damage counters, then says the source Pokemon is Knocked Out if the Ability is used.; CardImpl.h:1182-1183 and 1195-1196 register effectKoMe before the DamageCounter effect.; CreateCard.h:1657-1659 turns effectKoMe into the first Ko effect in the Ability effect vector.; EffectProc.h:798-821 and State.h:1695-1728 execute that vector in ascending effect-index order.; The exact engine therefore resolves self-KO before counter placement; this can expose a different intermediate public state and Prize-processing order.
- Recommendation: Use exact engine execution order because this registry targets the engine that generated the training games; preserve the natural-text discrepancy in evidence rather than changing runtime semantics.
- Human decision: Approved exact competition-engine execution order: self-KO first, then place the selected damage counters.
- Final status: resolved
